# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
## For College Students in India

*Prepared as part of CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project – VIT Bhopal University*

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Never click on links from unknown senders** — whether received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email. Always verify the source before clicking any link, especially those that prompt you to log in or enter personal details.

- [ ] **2. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password has at least 12 characters and includes a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed.

- [ ] **3. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts (email, social media, banking apps). This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

- [ ] **4. Keep your apps and operating system updated** — security patches fix known vulnerabilities that hackers exploit. Enable automatic updates wherever possible.

- [ ] **5. Avoid using public Wi-Fi** for sensitive activities like online banking, UPI transactions, or accessing college portals. If unavoidable, use a VPN.

- [ ] **6. Be sceptical of "too good to be true" offers** — fake scholarships, free recharges, prize winnings, and part-time job scams targeting students are very common. If it sounds too good to be true, it almost certainly is.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **7. Understand the difference between UPI "Send" and "Request"** — entering your UPI PIN confirms a *payment going out of your account*, not a payment coming in. You should NEVER need to enter your PIN to receive money.

- [ ] **8. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not with a bank representative, a seller, a buyer, or even a friend. No legitimate entity will ever ask for these details.

---

## Social Media & Personal Data Safety

- [ ] **9. Audit your privacy settings** on Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat at least once a semester. Ensure strangers cannot see your posts, location, or contact details.

- [ ] **10. Do not post photos of your ID cards, exam hall tickets, fee receipts, or mark sheets** online. These contain sensitive personal information that can be used for identity theft.

---

## If You Are a Victim – Reporting Channels

> 🚨 **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
> 
> 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (24×7, toll-free)

- Report immediately if you experience online fraud, cyberbullying, identity theft, or any digital crime.
- The faster you report financial fraud, the higher the chance of blocking the transaction.
- Keep screenshots and records of all conversations as evidence before reporting.
