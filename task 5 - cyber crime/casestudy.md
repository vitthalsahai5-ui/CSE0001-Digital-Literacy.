**Category:** Financial Cybercrime

## Case Study: "The Fake Refund Scam" – A UPI Fraud Scenario

### Background

Priya, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, sold a second-hand textbook on an online classifieds platform. Shortly after listing it, she received a message from a buyer who said he wanted to pay via PhonePe.

### Step-by-Step: How the Fraud Happened

1. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacted Priya claiming to be a genuine buyer and asked her UPI ID to send payment.

2. **The Trick:** Instead of initiating a payment, the fraudster sent a **UPI "Request Money" link** — which, when clicked and confirmed with a PIN, would transfer money *out* of Priya's account, not into it.

3. **Manipulation:** The fraudster told Priya: *"I've sent you ₹1,200. Check your UPI app — you just need to enter your PIN to confirm receipt."*

4. **The Victim's Mistake:** Priya, unfamiliar with how UPI worked, entered her PIN, believing she was accepting a payment. Instead, she authorised a transfer of ₹1,200 from her own account to the fraudster.

5. **Disappearance:** The fraudster immediately blocked Priya on all platforms and was untraceable.

### Who Is Usually Targeted

College students, elderly individuals, first-time online sellers, and people in rural or semi-urban areas who are less familiar with how UPI "request" vs "send" transactions work.

### Consequences

Priya lost ₹1,200 and suffered emotional distress. The fraudster was never caught. Filing a complaint took several days and caused significant academic disruption.

**Source:** This is a realistic fictional scenario based on a pattern of UPI fraud widely documented by the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and RBI advisories.
